## 1. Positioning Statement

**For** Israeli appointment-based service business owners (barbershops, salons, nail techs, dog groomers, clinics) who spend their days managing WhatsApp instead of their craft,

**Bapita** is a done-for-you digital operations service

**that** runs your WhatsApp, booking, Google presence, and client follow-ups — so you never miss a booking again and never have to learn a platform.

**Unlike** DIY automation platforms (Gambot, Lynxbe) that give you an engine and expect you to drive it, or marketplace platforms (Fresha, Nula) that take 20% commission on every client you already earned,

**Bapita** is the person who runs the digital side of your business — live in 48 hours, with one 30-minute call from you. That's it.

## 4. Brand Voice Rules

### The Bapita Voice in One Sentence
Direct, warm, Israeli peer — not a vendor pitching, a friend who happens to know exactly how to fix this.

### Rules

**1. Speak in real scenarios, not abstract benefits.**
Not: "Streamline your client communication."
Yes: "While you're mid-haircut, Bapita answers the booking request."

**2. Short sentences. Always.**
Max: 20 words per sentence in headlines. Cut every filler word.

**3. Loss-frame first, then solution.**
"You're losing ₪960/month in invisible missed bookings" before "Bapita costs ₪699."
Loss aversion is 2× more powerful than gain framing for this audience.

**4. Peer-to-peer tone.**
Never "our platform," never "solution," never "leverage."
Yes: "We run it for you," "you never touch it," "sounds like you."

**5. Hebrew-inflected warmth in English too.**
Slight informality is earned. Use "we" not "the system." Use names and real places when possible.

**6. Never mention AI in the headline or first screen.**
AI is the engine. Automation is the benefit. Human outcomes are the brand.

**7. Risk reversal always.**
Every CTA zone should have a risk reversal nearby: "If it's not working in 30 days, we refund the setup fee."

**8. "You never touch it" is the unlock phrase.**
This single idea dissolves the deepest objection. Use it often.

### Words to Use
`WhatsApp · appointment · booking · client · chair · while you're mid-haircut · after hours · never miss · from day one · we set it up · you never touch it · sounds like you · 48 hours · done`

### Words to Never Use
`AI-powered · chatbot · platform · CRM · automation suite · streamline · optimize · innovative · solution · leverage · enterprise · synergy · scalable · best-in-class`

## 5. Pricing Analysis & Validation - NOT FINAL _ (can be a little lower)

### The Proposed Tiers

| Tier | Price | Primary Buyer |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Starter | ₪699/mo | Solo barber/nail tech, 1 chair, testing the waters |
| No-Show Killer | ₪999/mo | Established solo operator bleeding no-shows |
| Full Autopilot | ₪1,490/mo | Multi-service / 2-3 staff, wants everything handled |
| Clinic Pack | ₪1,699/mo | Beauty clinic / physio / multi-room, higher ticket size |

### Market Benchmark

| Competitor | Price | Model | Why Bapita Wins |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Gambot | ₪3,500–12,000 one-time | DIY build | Setup cost alone > 6 months of Bapita Starter |
| Lynxbe | ₪499–1,500/mo | DIY SaaS | Same cost, zero setup help, no templates |
| Automaziot | ₪15,000+ one-time | Agency build | Disappears after build. No management. |
| WhatsApp operator (human) | ₪3,000–5,000/mo | Hired staff | 4-7× Bapita's price, takes sick days |
| Fresha/Nula | 20% commission | Marketplace | Eats revenue. Owners resent it fast. |
| vcita | ~$40–120/mo USD | DIY SaaS | USD pricing, no Hebrew-native WA, DIY |

### Validation by Tier

**Starter — ₪699/mo ✅ Strong**
- Below cost of 2 missed bookings/week (₪960/mo)
- Below Lynxbe DIY low end (₪499) when you factor setup + time
- Below ANY done-for-you alternative
- ROI proof: "One extra booking per week pays for it."
- Verdict: Right price. Don't discount it. Anchor against inaction.

**No-Show Killer — ₪999/mo ✅ Strong**
- Stays below the ₪1,000 psychological ceiling
- 1 empty chair/week × ₪150 avg = ₪600/mo loss. Reminder system prevents most of this.
- Name does the work: directly tied to the pain it solves
- Verdict: Price and name both work. Keep as-is.

**Full Autopilot — ₪1,490/mo ⚠️ Viable, needs stronger frame**
- 49% jump from No-Show Killer — largest gap in the stack
- Suitable for businesses generating ₪20,000+/mo (this is <8% of revenue)
- Consider: does this tier need a clear "included service list" that justifies the jump?
- Risk: without strong differentiation from ₪999, buyers may default down
- Recommendation: make tier inclusions explicit and add "dedicated account review" or "monthly strategy call" to justify the delta

**Clinic Pack — ₪1,699/mo ✅ Solid for vertical**
- Targets physio, beauty clinic, multi-room — higher ticket sizes (₪300–500/session)
- ROI math works: 1 filled session/month pays for it
- Only 14% premium over Full Autopilot — consider if this tier needs more exclusive features to hold the price
- Recommendation: bundle Google Ads management or a specific clinic-feature (intake forms, insurance field handling) to earn the premium

### Annual Pricing Recommendation 
Add annual option: 2 months free (≈17% discount). Frame as "2 free months" not "17% off."
- Starter annual: ₪6,990 (vs. ₪8,388) — saves ₪1,398
- No-Show Killer annual: ₪9,990 (vs. ₪11,988) — saves ₪1,998
Drives LTV, reduces churn, improves cash flow. Offer it as an upsell at end of 30-day trial.

### Setup Fee Note
₪1,200 setup fee is correct positioning — signals that real work is done, not just a login link. Don't remove it. Consider "free setup for annual plans" as an incentive.

### Price Anchoring Strategy (LP + Sales)
Always show the cost of inaction BEFORE showing Bapita's price:
> "2 missed bookings a week at ₪120 each = ₪960/month in invisible losses. Bapita Starter: ₪699/month."
> "Hiring someone just for WhatsApp: ₪3,000–5,000/month. Full Autopilot: ₪1,490/month."

## 7. Visual Identity Direction

*For Claude Design. Brief the designer from this section exactly.*

### Brand Feeling
Warm. Direct. Confident. Israeli. The friend who handles things — not the tech company that sells you software.

Think: a trusted local operator with real craft. Not a startup. Not a corporation. The kind of person you'd call when something needs to get done.

### What to Avoid (Hard Rules)
- Dark mode, circuit boards, robot imagery, neon — this is not a tech product
- Cold blue palettes — wrong emotional register
- Clipart of scissors, combs, or any single vertical icon — brand must work for barber AND physio AND dog groomer
- Generic "AI" visual language (brain icons, connected dots, etc.)
- English-only — Hebrew must feel native, not translated

---

### Color Palette Direction

**Primary — Warm Dominant**
Warm, earthy, energetic. Options to explore:
- Terra cotta / burnt sienna (₪ culture, Mediterranean warmth)
- Deep amber / saffron (energy, confidence, Israeli sun)
- Rich coral (warm, human, modern)
Choose ONE as primary. It should work on white AND on dark charcoal.

**Secondary — Grounded Dark**
Deep charcoal (#1a1a1a range) or warm navy. Grounded. Trustworthy. Pairs with the warm primary without feeling cold.

**Background — Cream / Warm White**
Avoid pure #FFFFFF — feels sterile. Use warm off-white or cream (#F7F4EF range) as the primary page background.

**Accent — Pop for CTAs**
A high-contrast accent for button states. Can be the primary color pushed to maximum saturation, or a warm yellow/saffron.

**Example palette to explore:**
- Primary: #D4622A (terra cotta)
- Dark: #1C1917 (warm near-black)
- Background: #F8F4EE (warm cream)
- Accent: #E8920A (amber)
- Light: #FDF0E4 (sand)

---

### Typography Direction

**Critical constraint:** All fonts MUST have Hebrew support (RTL). This is non-negotiable.

**Option A — Heebo-led (recommended for Hebrew-primary brand)**
- Headlines: Heebo Bold / ExtraBold (Google Fonts, free, excellent Hebrew)
- Body: Heebo Regular or Assistant Regular
- EN headlines: Heebo works in Latin script too — one font family, two scripts

**Option B — Rubik-led (more rounded, warmer feel)**
- Headlines: Rubik Bold
- Body: Rubik Regular
- More friendly, less authoritative than Heebo

**Option C — Mixed (Hebrew + EN pairing)**
- Hebrew: Heebo Bold (headlines), Assistant (body)
- EN: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold (headlines), Inter (body)
- Cleaner visual separation between scripts; more work to maintain

**Recommendation:** Option A or C. Avoid fonts without Hebrew support (most "cool" sans-serifs like Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk don't have Hebrew).

**Type scale principles:**
- Headlines: Large, bold, confident. Bapita's voice is not shy.
- Body: Readable at 16–18px. Never smaller than 15px.
- Caps: Use sparingly, only for labels/tags — Hebrew doesn't have uppercase.

---

### Logo Concepts (for designer to explore)

**Concept A — Wordmark only (recommended starting point)**
"bapita" in bold rounded sans-serif. Lowercase. Warm primary color on white, or white on dark.
The name is strong enough to carry the brand. Don't fight it with a complex mark.
Consider: a subtle ligature in the "pit" cluster, or a slight warmth-curve in the letter terminals.

**Concept B — Wordmark + abstract mark**
A simple enclosed/circular shape — referencing the pita idea (wrapped, complete, handled) without being literal.
Not: a pita bread illustration. Yes: a rounded enclosure or slight arc that feels like closure and completion.
The mark should work at 16px favicon size and at 300px social avatar size.

**Concept C — Bilingual lockup**
"bapita" (EN) with "בפיתה" (HE) stacked or side-by-side.
Works well if the primary market is Hebrew-speaking (it is). Signals cultural belonging immediately.
Keep it clean — don't stack more than 2 lines in the lockup.

**Logo don'ts:**
- No scissors, combs, phone icons, WhatsApp logos, or any vertical-specific imagery
- No gradient-heavy logo (ages fast, hard to reproduce on print/fabric)
- No tagline in the logo lockup — tagline lives in marketing materials, not the mark

---

### Iconography & Illustration Style

**Approach:** Minimal line icons, slightly rounded stroke. Not flat-filled, not 3D, not realistic.

**If illustrations are used:**
- Flat, warm-toned, human scenes. Think: person on phone (relaxed, not stressed), calendar filling up, WhatsApp chat bubbles turning into confirmed bookings.
- Characters should be ambiguously Israeli — olive skin, casual dress, Mediterranean context. Not generic stock art.
- Style reference direction: somewhere between Oat Studio warmth and Blush Design simplicity. Human, not corporate.

---

### Spacing & Layout Principles

**Generous whitespace.** This brand is about clarity and calm — the opposite of the client's current chaos.
**Mobile-first.** Primary audience uses phones, not desktops. Every layout decision starts mobile.
**Hebrew RTL first.** The Hebrew version is the primary experience. English is the documentation/international layer.
**Bold section headers.** Big, confident, generous leading. Don't be timid with type.

---

### UI / Component Personality (for web)

- Rounded corners (12–16px border-radius) — warm, not sterile
- Buttons: Filled primary color, bold text, generous padding (14px+ vertical)
- Cards: Warm cream background, subtle shadow, no harsh borders
- Form inputs: Clean, no drop shadows, clear focus states in primary color
- Avoid: flat gray UI, sharp corners, enterprise dashboard feel
