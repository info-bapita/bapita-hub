# Bapita — Sales Cheat Sheet
*Last updated: June 2026 · Herzliya barbershops · Door-to-door + Instagram + Calls*

---

## Before You Walk In

- Personalize demo with their name, photos, real services (1 hour)
- Test booking flow end-to-end on your phone
- Have demo URL open and ready before you enter
- Best timing: Tuesday–Thursday, 12:00–15:00 (slowest for barbers)
- Demo: `book.bapita.com/[slug]`

---

## Step 1 — Read the Room First (Always)

Ask before pitching. Two questions tell you everything:

**Q1: "How do you handle bookings right now?"**

| Answer | Their pain | Your angle |
|---|---|---|
| "WhatsApp" | DM chaos, late nights | Automation, stop answering at midnight |
| "Phone calls" | Misses calls while cutting | Never miss a booking, works 24/7 |
| "Fresha / Booksy" | Paying commissions | Flat fee, no cuts, your own domain |
| "Everything's fine" | No felt pain | Image upgrade + no-shows + new clients |

**Q2: "Do you ever get no-shows?"**
- Yes → no-show reminders are a real selling point
- No / rarely → skip it, don't invent pain that isn't there

## Step 3 — Demo Flow

1. Hand or hold phone so they can see
2. **Say nothing while they scroll** — silence is not awkward, it's effective
3. Then: "This is what your clients see — services, calendar, they pick a slot, it's booked. Under a minute. No DMs, no back and forth."
4. Show dashboard briefly: "And you see everything here — today's schedule, client list, revenue."
5. Don't oversell features. Show → ask → listen.

## Step 4 — Objection Bank

### "I use Fresha / Booksy"
> "Fresha takes a percentage of every payment. Booksy charges per booking on top of the monthly fee. Bapita is flat — ₪150/month right now, unlimited bookings, no commissions ever. And your page is yours — not inside a marketplace next to your competitors."

---

### "Too expensive / no budget"
> "₪150 a month is less than one haircut. No setup fee. And it works while you're sleeping."

> "One extra client a month from your booking link covers the whole year. Most barbers get 5–10 new bookings in the first month just from putting the link in their Instagram bio."

> "And you get my personal WhatsApp. Something breaks, you call me, fixed same day. You're not paying for software — you're paying for someone who manages it for you."

---

### "I have a notebook / I manage fine on WhatsApp"
> "This isn't for you — it's for your clients. You keep the notebook, nothing changes for you. But 8 out of 10 people prefer to book online. The ones who can't be bothered to DM you? They find someone who has a booking link. This just catches them."

---

### "My clients know me / I don't need this"
> "Your regulars — yes, they're loyal. This is for the new person who found your Instagram, loved the work, but didn't want to DM a stranger. They want to just pick a time and book. That's who you're missing right now."

---

### "I'm already on Google Maps / people find me"
> "They find you — great. But right now they have to call or WhatsApp to book. Half of them won't bother. With Bapita, your Google listing has a 'Book Now' button — they find you, they book in 30 seconds. You're already visible. This makes that visibility actually convert."

---

### "I already have a website"
> "Does it take bookings? Not just a contact form — actual time slots, confirmation, the whole flow?"

- If no: "That's what this does. Your current site is a brochure. Bapita is a booking machine."
- If yes: "What do you use? I can show you what's different."

---

### "What if you shut down / disappear?"
> "Fair question. The booking page and all client data belong to you — I can export it any time. The domain is yours. If Bapita ever closed, you'd keep everything. And I'm not going anywhere — I have clients depending on this."

---

### "My clients are old, they don't use the internet"
> "Some do, some don't. The ones who don't — nothing changes, they still call or WhatsApp you. For anyone who does use a phone to search, you're now bookable. You lose no one. You only gain."

---

### "I tried something like this before and it didn't work"
First ask: "What did you try?" — listen before responding.

Then: "The difference is I build it for you, I set it up, it's live in 48 hours. You don't touch anything. Most things fail because the owner has to manage it themselves. This you don't."

---

### "I'm too busy / not now"
> "Let me build a version with your actual name and photos — takes me 2 days, costs you nothing to look at. If you don't love it, zero obligation. When can I send it?"

---

### "I need to think about it / talk to my wife"
> "Of course. I'll build the preview with your name on it — free — and send it over. You look at it together, if you both like it, we go. Nothing to sign right now."

## Step 5 — Stats (One Only, After They're Interested)

Use to solidify, never to open. One stat lands — three sounds like a sales deck.

**Best one:**
> "8 out of 10 people prefer a business where they can book online. Right now only 1 in 4 barbers offer it. You'd be ahead of almost everyone in Herzliya."

**No-show angle:**
> "Automated reminders drop no-show rates by 30–40%. One extra slot filled a week pays for the whole thing."

## Step 6 — Close Sequence

**Always offer the preview first (lowest friction):**
> "Let me build the full version with your actual photos — free, 48 hours. You see it, you love it, we go. Zero cost to look."

**When they're clearly ready — first customer offer:**
> "I'm building my first client list right now. No setup fee — normally ₪2,500, I'm waiving it. Just ₪150 a month. And you get my personal WhatsApp — if anything ever breaks, you message me directly, I fix it same day. I can have it live by [today + 2 days]. Want to go?"

**Price anchoring:**
> "Booksy charges per booking on top of their fee. Fresha takes a cut of every payment. Bapita is ₪150 flat — no setup, no commissions, unlimited bookings."

**Direct support angle (use when they worry about tech / trust):**
> "You get my personal number. Not a support ticket, not a chatbot — me. Something breaks, you WhatsApp me, done same day."

**If price is still the blocker:**
> "₪150 a month is less than one haircut. If you don't see value in 30 days, we stop. No contract, no lock-in."

## After the Meeting

**Interested but didn't close:**
1. Build personalized preview within 24hrs
2. WhatsApp: "Built your preview — [link]. Let me know what you think."
3. Follow up once after 48hrs if no reply. Then move on.

**Closed:**
1. Collect first month ₪150 (PayPal / Bit for now) — no setup fee for early clients
2. Add business in admin: `dashboard.bapita.com/admin/businesses`
3. Deploy in 48hrs
4. Send link + 30-min walkthrough call
5. First instruction: "Put this link in your Instagram bio and Stories. That's step one."

**Hard no:**
- "No problem. If anything changes, I'm here." Leave number.
- Log the shop — don't revisit too soon.

## Game Day — Mindset + Sequence

**Goal = 10 doors, not 10 sales.** You collect reactions, not closes. 10 doors entered = win, even if all say no. You can't fail at walking in. Closing isn't in your control — volume is.

**Why you don't feel ready:** "Ready" arrives *after* shop #1, never before. First door cures the fear. Prep can't.

**Tonight (30 min, then stop):**
1. Build ONE personalized demo — real name, photos, services from their Instagram
2. Test booking flow on phone, screenshot as backup
3. Say the 2 Hebrew openers out loud 5x
4. Charge phone. Done. Stop polishing.

**Morning:**
1. Start with shop you care *least* about — burn nerves on a low-stakes door
2. First line, then **shut up**: "הבעלים בפנים?" → "בניתי לך דף הזמנות, רגע?" → phone out, silent
3. Mid-cut = gift: "אין בעיה, אני חוזר בעוד שעה" → soft appointment, come back
4. Every no → log the objection that killed it. That's day-2 ammo.

**Mindset:** Not a beggar interrupting. You built them something free, name on it, before they asked. You're doing *them* a favor. Walk in like it. Barbers respect hustle.

**Go at noon.** Don't let morning rot into more prep. Prep is done.
