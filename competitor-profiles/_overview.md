## 3. Homepage Redesign Suggestions

### Current issues with v3 homepage:

| Issue | Why it's a problem |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Hero too generic** | "Run your business online" could be any SaaS. No hook. |
| **No numbers/social proof** | No "X businesses using Bapita", no testimonials visible |
| **5 products shown immediately** | Options overload. SMBs want *one* solution to their *one* problem. |
| **"No agency needed"** | Weak positioning. They'd benefit from "We *are* your agency but at SaaS prices." |
| **No visual storytelling** | All text, no screenshots, no product mockups, no demo |
| **Pricing before trust** | Users see prices before they see any proof it works |
| **"Coming soon" products dilute focus** | 4 of 5 products are "coming soon" — page feels like vaporware |
| **No live demo / interactive element** | Nothing to click and experience |
| **Weak CTAs** | "See the tools" and "How it works" — not action-oriented |
| **Mobile hero** | Long headline wraps oddly, no visual break |

### Suggested redesign approach:

#### A. Hero — Hook hard on pain + outcome

Current:
```
Run your business online. No agency needed.
```

Suggested:
```
Stop wrestling with software. We build and run your business tools — so you don't have to.
```

Then sub-text that leads with the *one thing* they came for. And a screenshot or visual of an actual booking page / dashboard. Make it real.

#### B. "As seen on" / trust bar BEFORE products

Move the social proof strip up and give it more weight. Show logos of actual businesses or recognizable categories with counts.

Add: Join [X] businesses already using Bapita Book.

#### C. Featured product — hero section CTA goes to the LIVE product

4/5 products are "coming soon." That's a credibility problem. Solution: Make "Book" the hero story. Lead with the thing that works. Mention the suite as the longer vision.

#### D. Replace 5-product grid with problem/solution layout

Instead of 5 product cards, use:
```
[Problem 1] → [Tool that solves it]  (Book — live)
[Problem 2] → [Tool that solves it]  (Social — coming soon)
...
```
Each row has before/after storytelling. Shows you understand their pain.

#### E. Add a working demo / calculator / interactive element

Something low-friction. A "See your booking page" mockup preview. Or a 3-step config wizard that shows the output changing in real time.

#### F. Testimonials section with real names/photos

Even if you have 3 clients. Authentic testimonials beat generic social proof every time.

#### G. Founder video or "Why we built this" section

For a product with this positioning (we do it for you), trust is everything. A short founder message builds that.

#### H. Pricing — flip order on page

Put pricing *after* you've convinced them. Current order: Hero → Social proof → Products → How It Works → Who It's For → Pricing. That's good actually. Just remove "Coming soon" badges from pricing cards — they kill urgency.

#### I. Visual language
- Add product mockups/screenshots throughout
- Use color-coded section transitions (each section gets a subtle accent from one product color)
- More whitespace, less text density

#### J. One ask CTA

Instead of 4 CTAs ("See tools", "How it works", "Get notified", "See plans"), have a primary CTA everywhere: **"Book a free call"** — this aligns with the white-glove positioning.
